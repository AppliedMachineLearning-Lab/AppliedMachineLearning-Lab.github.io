## About me

Dr. Corinna Schmalohr is a Data Scientist and Bioinformatician specializing in applying artificial intelligence and machine learning to biomedical and clinical research. She studied Biomedicine and Computational Biology and completed her PhD in Computational Biology at the University of Cologne. In September 2025, she joined the Applied Machine Learning Lab in Bonn as a postdoctoral researcher.

Her research focuses on developing AI-driven methods to analyze complex clinical, genomic, and patient-generated data with the goal of improving diagnosis, understanding disease mechanisms, and supporting precision medicine. She is particularly interested in combining machine learning with domain knowledge to create interpretable, reliable, and clinically relevant models.

Dr. Schmalohr’s work bridges computational innovation and medical application, advancing data-driven approaches to tackle challenges in rare diseases, biomedical research, and patient care.

## Research Interests

- **AI and machine learning for clinical and biomedical applications**

- **Predictive modeling and decision support in healthcare**

- **Analysis of heterogeneous and multimodal medical datasets**
